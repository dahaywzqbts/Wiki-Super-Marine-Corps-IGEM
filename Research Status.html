Research Status

Specific recognition of microplastics in the environment

Commonly used MPs identification techniques include visual and spectroscopic methods, such as Raman spectroscopy, Fourier transform infrared (FT-IR) spectroscopy, and pyrolysis-gas chromatography-mass spectrometry (Py-GC-MS). Among them, Py-GC-MS was proposed by Fischer et al., this technique does not rely on physical preselection or microplastic appearance, and can analyze eight common plastic types, complementary to FT-IR and Raman methods. Lin et al. studied the application progress of microplastic recognition in the machine learning technology environment, indicating that the combination of machine learning and traditional spectroscopic technology can significantly improve the accuracy and efficiency of microplastic recognition. Gong et al. proposed a novel microfluidic device for label-free identification of microplastics in seawater, which improved the efficiency and accuracy of analysis by combining microfluidic techniques, Raman spectroscopy and machine learning techniques.


The upgrade of plastic reengineering

The upgrading of plastic reengineering can be explained as the use of plastic waste as the raw material for synthetic value-added products. At present, the research trend at home and abroad focuses on the upgrading and reengineering of plastics through chemical recycling technology. —— including the deconstruction, reconstruction and functionalization of polymers. Sullivan et al. proposed the conversion of mixed plastics into valuable products through series catalysis and biological funnel (Figure 1). Austin et al studied the PETase enzyme, which improved its degradation efficiency on PET. A method of near-complete depolymerization of polyester using nanodispersion enzymes was proposed by DelRe et al., which is important for studying the biodegradation and recycling of polymers. In China, QIAN and others discussed the application of synthetic biology in the biological depolymerization and upgrading of waste plastics, including the mining of plastic-degrading microorganisms and enzymes, the design and construction of mixed bacteria / multi-enzyme systems, the plastic depolymerization mechanism, and the process of transforming plastic depolymerization into high value-added products.
