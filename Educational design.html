Educational design

Summary of scientific knowledge

Prepare educational materials suitable for the public of all ages, including animated videos, interactive exhibitions and easy-to-understand popular science articles, mainly including:

① Source of microplastics.
② definition of microplastics.
③ hazards of microplastics (or plastic) contamination.
④ Introduction, scientific principles and objectives of our project.


Specific activity plan

    ① Campus lecture and public account publicity (target primary and secondary schools, high schools, universities): cooperate with the school college to provide students with lectures on microplastic pollution and synthetic biology, and through the public account publicity.

    ② Community presentation (targeted middle-aged and elderly people): Teach how to identify microplastics in life and promote the concept of environmental protection.

    ③ Online games: Design and launch mini-games related to microplastics through social media platforms to attract the public in an interesting form.

Educational expectations

    ① Raise public awareness of microplastics problems.

    ② Enhance society's confidence and support for synthetic biology's ability to solve environmental problems.

    ③ Stimulate the younger generation's interest in scientific research and environmental protection.

Conclusion

    Address the question of how our projects affect the world around us and how the world around us affects our projects through a use of human practice and education. We believe that through continuous efforts and cooperation from all sectors of society, we can jointly face and solve the global challenge of microplastic pollution.
