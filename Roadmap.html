Specific identification of environmental microplastics:

FTIR

Identify the chemical bonds of specific substances to determine the type of MPS. However, measuring factors such as the shape, size, color and the simultaneous presence of multiple polymers can interfere with the results and hinder the identification.


Raman spectrum

Using photon scattering technology, backscattered light with different frequencies is generated according to the different molecular structure and atoms of the sample, obtaining unique spectral images of different polymers. Raman can image the samples with high resolution chemical resolution as low as 500nm without interference from the shape, size or thickness of the measured particles.




Transformation and application of multi-enzyme system bax

Primer design

Four primers were designed with SnapGene (Table I), namely, Is-F, Is-R, E6-F, and E6-R. And BamH I and EcoRI restriction endonuclease restriction sites were introduced at the 5 ′ end of primers Is-F and E6-R, respectively, and rigid peptide Linker was added at the 5 ′ end of primers Is-R and E6-F.



Construction of the fusion gene

ISF6_4831 (NCIB, GI: 908203773) as template, Is-F / Is-R as primer; similarly, Comamonas sp. Strain E6 (NCIB, GI: 78210734) as template and E6-F / E6-R as primer. The amplified products were detected by 1% agarose gel electrophoresis of the identified products and the ISF6_4831-TPADO fusion gene using the ISF6_4831 gene and the Is-F / E6-R as the primer, as the template.


Construction of the recombinant plasmids

The BamH I and EcoRI restriction enzymes cut pET-28a (+) empty-vector, recovered the ISF6_4831-E6 gene fragment and linearized vector pET-28a (+), respectively, ligated with T4 ligase, again by double digestion with BamH I and EcoRI restriction nuclease, verified the correct recombinant transfer plasmid for sequencing, and named pET-28a-Is-E6.





Transfection of the recombinant plasmids

Plasmid pET-28a-Is-E6 was transfected to E. coli DH 5 α using the transfection reagent. Plasmids were mixed with DH 5 α competent cells at appropriate ratios and transfected under appropriate conditions for a certain period of time. The transfected cells were cultured in the medium containing kanamycin to see whether the colonies could grow and whether the transfection was successful.


Protein purification

Successfully transfected cells were fragmented and centrifuged to remove impurities such as cell debris. Ni2 + affinity resin was prepared and Ni2 + affinity resin was combined with the protein. The bound proteins were then eluted with buffer to obtain the purified his-tag protein. The purified enzyme was cultured on medium containing PET and tested whether its product had PCA to determine whether it was successfully expressed and active.


Molecular docking

Studying interactions between enzymes by molecular docking and predict their binding mode and affinity, etc.


Kinetic simulation and experimental studies of enzyme-catalyzed reactions

By controlling the substrate concentration, pH value and temperature of the reaction system, the catalytic effect and the optimal catalytic condition of PET as PAC were explored.
