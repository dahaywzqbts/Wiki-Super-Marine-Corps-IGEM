Human practice

As the problem of microplastic pollution is receiving increasing attention worldwide, our team is committed to developing a solution for the specific identification
of microplastics in the environment and the upgrading and reconstruction of plastics. The following explains how we are prepared to ensure a socially responsible
and positive impact of our programs through human practice activities, while popularizing the science behind synthetic biology through innovative educational
tools and promotions.

Research and interview

In order to have a deep understanding of the current situation of microplastics and the concerns of all sectors of society, we will continue to improve our project by:
① raising questions and ideas.
② communicating with stakeholders for feedback.
③ evaluating project ideas and design.
④ concluding and thus improving the project.



We started with the research and interview activities:

① Through the questionnaire survey to understand and collect the public to micro plastics, micro plastics pollution and plastic treatment.

② Try and talk with relevant environmental organizations, relevant research institutions and teams, representatives of plastic manufacturers, and policy makers to collect valuable comments and suggestions to help us optimize project design and ensure their social responsibility and practical application value.

③ Discuss with professors in biosynthetic or environmental direction research, assessing the feasibility and originality of each project idea and the required expertise.

④ Discuss with the instructor, consider the school's experimental conditions and financial support, to ensure the development of the project.



Public popularization and communication

① Organize online and offline public lectures.

② Social media activities, with short video publicity and media coverage and dissemination. Populto the public the severity of microplastic pollution and the potential of synthetic biology in solving such problems. Raise public awareness of microplastics issues, inform the public of our project, and stimulate support for it.
